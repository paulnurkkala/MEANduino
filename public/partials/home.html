What is MEANduino?

MEANduino is a simple boilerplate MEAN application that is intended to serve as a training/learning application for those that want to connect their arduino to the internet of things. It's a free, easy, and fast way to set up a web-server for your Arduino application. Basically, by using various free softwares and services, we are hoping to help individuals that aren't generally web-programmers connect their creations to the internet. Once you have the ability to easily push your data to the internet, you have many more opportunities than you would otherwise have.

Where can I get it?

The MEANduino "stack" is available at the github page here: here. Tutorials and other information will be made avaiable to help you set up the free services and take your creation to the next level.

What can I do with MEANduino?

Any. Thing. You want.

That's the point and the fun of what we've done. We've created a bare-bones simple, example-driven "boilerplate" MEAN application that you can use for free to start pushing your information online. If you sit down, take a few minutes to walk through the well documented and explained code, you'll have enough of an understanding to create your own web-enabled Arduino data gathering apps.

Please also visit our demo page for an example of a MEANduino-based application!
